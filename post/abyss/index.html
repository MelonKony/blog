Moments in the Abyss

By Hobart Phillips on March 25, 2021
blog

Sometimes I just need a short-form space to put down some thoughts. This isn’t really a public thing. I’m not starting a newsletter. Most introspection is done introspectively. But sometimes it’s just easier to write it out.

This week, I started waking up around 4:45am each day. It’s a better way to live. Previously, Tzipora was a late-sleeper – I think, considering how much better it is living alongside the sun, it makes more sense if she’s an early riser. She has that stern social conservatism about her, so it only makes sense.

Some notes to myself, while I’m here.

  • The comic is going pretty horribly. Not that it’s coming out bad, just that it’s not really coming out at all. Who knew it was so hard to do what people spend a lifetime doing?
  • I want to restart the Atlantic Bulletin. Properly, as a lengthy periodical. The project gets boring otherwise. A lot of people like the academic stuff, I’m just not well-read enough to keep up.
  • I need to structure my days better. Actually, let’s expand on that.

I had an atrocious time today trying to implement a ‘reader view’ on the website. Actually, that’s part of why I founded this blog. A whole day wasted with that, and I’m already behind on both my job-hunting and regular illustrations. I don’t even want to think about the comic.

It wasn’t all bad. I had a parmie at the pub and cooked pasta for dinner. I also took the cat to the vet – she’s doing well üê±. I feel pretty rubbish otherwise. That’s all you can do to describe it really, and you still sound like a piece of shit saying it. There really is no way to write things down without sounding pathetically self-concerned. Maybe I’ll have to open each one of these with apologies.

I sent Captain V. Shemozzle’s three albums. I love them, it reminds me of being a kid. I used to like the high-energy stuff that everyone would dance to down in Salamanca, but recently I’ve been listening to Yahoudi’s Waltz. I’ll try and link that below.

Enjoy the song. See you around.
